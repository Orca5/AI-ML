Observations¶

Average vocal fundamental frequency has non uniform distribution.¶
  1. There are more number of individuals with frequencies roughly between 110Hz and 120Hz.
  2. There are less number of individuals greater than around 210Hz
  3. The rest of the frequencies have individuals between 10 to 30 nos
Maximum vocal fundamental frequency has highly skewed distribution¶
  1. Majority of the individuals are spread between 100Hz and 300Hz
  2. The rest of them are spread between 350Hz to 580Hz
  3. It is interesting to see approximately 300Hz to 350Hz and 480Hz to 550Hz there are no individuals
Minimum vocal fundamental frequecy has a skewed distribution¶
  1. Almost 1/3rd of the population is spread between 50Hz to 120Hz
  2. The rest of the population is fairly distributed between 120Hz to approx. 250Hz

It has to be further examined to identify a pattern among these distributions.

# Logistic Regression - non scaled version

from sklearn.metrics import classification_report, confusion_matrix, roc_auc_score, roc_curve, accuracy_score

from sklearn.linear_model import LogisticRegression

# Fit the model on train

lr.fit(x_train, y_train)

from sklearn import metrics

lr_train_predict = lr.predict(x_train)
lr_test_predict = lr.predict(x_test)


print("Model Accuracy for Training Data: {0:.4f}".format(metrics.accuracy_score(y_train, lr_train_predict)))
print("Model Accuracy for Testing Data: {0:.4f}".format(metrics.accuracy_score(y_test, lr_test_predict)))

# Accuracy Score
